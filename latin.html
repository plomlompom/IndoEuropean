    classical

    -o- becomes -u- before final consonants (Sihler 1995 p. 66), nominative/vocative -ros/-re becomes -r after vowels and -er after consonants (Sihler 1995 p. 70), vocative -ie and genitive -iī become -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 256, 260), dative -ōi and ablative -ōd become -ō (Sihler 1995 p. 58, 228), word-final -ns becomes -s with lengthening of preceding vocal (Sihler 1995 p. 215), -ā becomes -a (Sihler 1995 p. 78), introduction of singular genitive in -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 260), -ois becomes -eis becomes -īs (Sihler 1995 p. 263), same with -āis (Sihler 1995 p. 272), o-stem genitive plural partly modeled after a-stem (Sihler 1995 p. 264), o-stem nominative plural is replaced from the pronoun -oi becoming -ei becoming -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 260), same with a-stem nominative plural (Sihler 1995 p. 271), long vowels before -m got short (Sihler 1995 p. 78), -āi shortened to -ae (Sihler 1995 p. 268), loss of word-final -d after a long vowel (Sihler 1995 p. 228), in a-stem plural dative-ablative –īs is replaced with -ābus where necessary to avoid homophony with o-stem plural dative-ablatives (Sihler 1995 p. 272), intervocalic -s becomes -r (Sihler 1995 p. 171-172), word-final -ss becomes -s (Sihler 1995 p. 204-205), dorsal stop plus -s becomes -x (Sihler 1995 p. 204). A locative plural in -īs is too speculative (Sihler 1995 p. 253 only mentions it, prefixed with a "perhaps") to include in the table.
