Latin

Note "(<)" abbreviates "(fused with last stem sound (consonant or vowel))", "(<<)" abbreviates "(fused with last stem vowel + consonant)".

proto-italic

Note that Sihler 1995 p. 286 names -fos as the Proto-Italic dative-ablative consonant stem plural ending, but this (even if reflected in all other Italic languages) is inconsistent with Early Latin's reflex of original -bʰos as -bos (Sihler 1995 p. 147, 286), so the earlier form is here given.

    classical

    -o- becomes -u- before final consonants (Sihler 1995 p. 66), -e- becomes -i- before non-nasal consonants (general rule with some exception, but holds true before any final consnants) (Sihler 1995 p. 65), nominative/vocative -ros/-re becomes -r after vowels and -er after consonants (Sihler 1995 p. 70), vocative -ie and genitive -iī become -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 256, 260), dative -ōi and ablative -ōd become -ō (Sihler 1995 p. 58, 228), word-final -ns becomes -s with lengthening of preceding vocal (Sihler 1995 p. 215), -ā becomes -a (Sihler 1995 p. 78), introduction of singular genitive in -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 260), -ois becomes -eis becomes -īs (Sihler 1995 p. 263), same with -āis (Sihler 1995 p. 272), o-stem genitive plural partly modeled after a-stem (Sihler 1995 p. 264), o-stem nominative plural is replaced from the pronoun -oi becoming -ei becoming -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 260), same with a-stem nominative plural (Sihler 1995 p. 271), long vowels before -m got short (Sihler 1995 p. 78), -āi shortened to -ae (Sihler 1995 p. 268), loss of word-final -d after a long vowel (Sihler 1995 p. 228), in a-stem plural dative-ablative –īs is replaced with -ābus where necessary to avoid homophony with o-stem plural dative-ablatives (Sihler 1995 p. 272), intervocalic -s becomes -r (Sihler 1995 p. 171-172), word-final -ss becomes -s (Sihler 1995 p. 204-205), dorsal stop plus -s becomes -x (Sihler 1995 p. 204), -ei- in final syllable becomes -ī (Sihler 1995 p. 70), final -i becomes -e (Sihler 1995 p. 65), either the consonant locative or (less likely) instrumental singular becomes the consonant ablative singular (Sihler 1995 p. 285), consonant locative singular in -ī is modeled in analogy to o-stems (Sihler 1995 p. 285), the -bus derived from the -b- reflex to word-internal -bʰ- (Sihler 1995 p. 145, 147) and got an -i- prefix (Sihler 1995 p. 286), the i-stem plural nominative ending -ēs becomes the consonant plural nominative ending proper (Sihler 1995 p. 286), [-rs becomes -rr becomes -r (Sihler 1995 p. 211), but this is not needed due to p. 290] former root stems in -s whose -s- became -r- intervocalic retain -s in the nominative singular (Sihler 1995 p. 310-311), long vowels before final non-s consonants shorten (Sihler 1995 p. 79), the -on- suffix of consonant stems is reflected as medial -in- (Sihler 1995 p. 60-61, 295-296), certain animate n-stem nouns with nominative singular in -en emerge from gender change, compounds with the weakened root "can-", and unclear etymology (Sihler 1995 p.296). Not depicted: kinship nouns of animate consonant stems in -r with nominative singular in -er (<) (like "pater"/"patris") (Sihler 1995 p. 293). Not depicted: The consonant stems in -n- freak cases of "carō"/"carnis", "iuvenis"/"iuvenis", "sangu(i|ī)s"/"sanguinis", "liēn"/"li(e|ē)nis", "flāmen"/"flāminis", "pecten"/"pectinis", (Sihler 1995 p. 291, 295-296). A locative plural in -īs is too speculative (Sihler 1995 p. 253 alone mentions it, prefixed with a "perhaps") to include in the table.
